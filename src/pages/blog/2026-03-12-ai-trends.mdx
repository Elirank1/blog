Two things happened this week that, taken together, define the current moment in AI better than any single announcement could.

First: Anthropic opened the "Agent Skills" standard — making agent capabilities portable across platforms, not locked to Claude. On the same day, Microsoft announced Copilot Cowork, a desktop-native agentic knowledge work tool built in partnership with Anthropic. The message is clear. Anthropic is not building a product company. It's building infrastructure for the agentic era, and it wants enterprise distribution yesterday.

Second: Apple shipped Gemini-powered Siri. Not OpenAI. Google. To two billion devices. That deal tells you more about where we are than any benchmark could.

Here is what you need to understand: the battle for the AI coordination layer is not being fought at the model level. It is being fought at the integration level — who gets embedded, who becomes default, who earns the right to act on your behalf without you having to think about it.

Anthropic is betting on portable skills. Apple is betting on platform distribution. The winner is whoever makes AI feel less like a tool you pick up and more like a layer you live inside.

That is exactly what Deeplica is building. Not a smarter assistant. A coordination layer.
